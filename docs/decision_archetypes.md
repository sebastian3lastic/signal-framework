# Signal Framework \- Decision Archetypes

This document describes recurring decision environments where Signal Framework tends to be particularly useful.

These are **not marketing personas**. They represent patterns of decision-making under uncertainty.

Different roles. Different geographies. The same structural tension: too much change, too little explicit decision logic.

⸻

1. ## Martín \- The structured operator

   ***Buenos Aires, Argentina***  
   Martín is 30\. He studied Industrial Engineering and works as a Project Manager in a banking-related environment.

   His daily work involves dashboards, stakeholder updates, vendor pitches, and operational trade-offs. Every few weeks, a new tool is proposed internally — often presented as urgent or transformative.

   He does not fear innovation. He fears unjustified decisions.

   His main constraint is accountability. If he adopts something today, he may need to defend that decision six months later in front of finance, compliance, or leadership.

   ### What he needs

* Explicit criteria  
* Clear numerical thresholds  
* Decisions that can be explained and documented

  ### Why Signal helps

  Signal transforms enthusiasm into a structured evaluation. It replaces “this looks promising” with “this meets (or does not meet) the defined threshold.”

  For Martín, Signal is not about moving faster. It is about moving defensibly.

  ⸻

2. ## Aoife \- The systems thinker

   ***Cork, Ireland***  
   Aoife is 45 and a senior product designer specializing in design systems.

   She has experienced multiple cycles of hype. She has inherited fragmented tooling decisions made in moments of urgency.

   She does not resist change. She resists fragmentation.

   Her concern is structural coherence:

* Does this create lock-in?  
* Does this introduce long-term maintenance costs?  
* Does it align with system evolution rather than isolated productivity gains?

  ### What she needs

* Clear separation between opportunity and risk  
* Explicit treatment of structural risk  
* A disciplined way to experiment without destabilizing the system

  ### Why Signal helps

  Signal distinguishes positive criteria from risk-based criteria. It enforces veto rules for non-compensatory risks like lock-in or privacy exposure.

  For Aoife, Signal is not about avoiding innovation. It is about innovating responsibly.

  ⸻

3. ## Arjun — The emerging strategist

   ***Pune, India***  
   Arjun is 23 and finishing his degree in Industrial Engineering.

   He is surrounded by startups, hackathons, AI communities, and a constant stream of new tools. His feeds move faster than his calendar.

   He experiments frequently. He bookmarks even more.

   His challenge is not access to innovation. It is prioritization.

   Everything feels relevant. Everything feels urgent.

   ### What he needs

* A way to anchor decisions to a defined objective  
* A visible representation of trade-offs   
* Permission to ignore tools that do not serve his direction

  ### Why Signal helps 

  Signal forces the definition of a 6-month objective before evaluation. It makes opportunity cost visible.

  For Arjun, Signal is not a restriction. It is a training ground for judgment.


4. ## Lucía — Founder under pressure

***Madrid, Spain***  
Lucía is building a product in a small team.

She moves fast because she has to. Cash runway, investor expectations, and market timing shape her decisions.

Every week, she is exposed to:

* New AI tools promising leverage  
* Growth hacks  
* Infrastructure shortcuts  
* “Scale instantly” narratives  
    
  Her real tension is not innovation.  
  It is survival with coherence.  
    
  Adopting the wrong tool can mean:  
* Hidden lock-in  
* Escalating costs  
* Rewriting infrastructure  
* Operational fragility  
    
  But ignoring everything can mean falling behind.

  ### What she needs

* A way to evaluate leverage vs long-term cost  
* Clear visibility of structural risk  
* A disciplined experiment mode before committing

  ### Why Signal helps

  Signal forces her to ask:

* Does this materially improve my next 6 months?  
* Does this increase or reduce scaling risk?  
* If this fails, how painful is the rollback?  
    
  The veto logic protects against irreversible mistakes.  
  The Sandbox mode allows exploration without destabilizing production.  
    
  For Lucía, Signal is not about slowing down.  
    
  It is about scaling without accidental fragility.

  ⸻

## Beyond Roles

These archetypes are not exclusive user segments.

They represent recurring patterns of decision pressure: 

* High accountability  
* High system complexity  
* High learning velocity  
* High survival risk  
    
  If you operate in any environment where change outpaces reflection, Signal may be relevant.

## Shared Pattern

Across all three contexts: 

* Exposure to rapid technological change  
* Increasing cognitive load  
* Pressure to adopt tools quickly  
* Limited time to reflect structurally

  Signal Framework does not eliminate uncertainty.

  It formalizes how decisions are made within it.
